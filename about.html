About Suzanne Collins

Suzanne Collins is an American author and screen writer best know for the popular trilogy called the Hunger Games. In her early life, Collins studied theatre and telecommunications at Indiana University. After graduating, Collins wrote for the 1990s sitcom Hi Honey, Im home! As well as for the animated children’s series called Clifford's Puppy Days. Collins shifted her focus form screen writing to novels; releasing her first children book in 2003 titled Gregor the Overlander. Soon after, Collins released the Underland Chronicles and the hunger games - both of which were inspired by the Vietnam war. The release of the Hunger Games put Collins on the map - naming her one of Time Magazines most influential people in 2010, as well as the all time best selling author on the Amazon Kindle. later, the series was adapted by Lisonsgate and turned into a movie.

In September 2013, Collins released a critically acclaimed autobiographical picture book called Year of The Jungle. Like her other series, The Year of The Jungle touches on the Vietnam war, and her childhood experiences of her father being deployed. Collectively, Collins deals with difficult topics in fantastical ways. She bring new and fresh perspectives to her books. Selling over 100 million copies of her work worldwide — Suzanne Collins has certainly become a household name.
